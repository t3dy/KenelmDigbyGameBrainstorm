Sir Kenelm Digby and the Power of Sympathy
Seth Lobis
abstract In A Late Discourse.  .  .  . Touching the Cure of Wounds by the Powder
of Sympathy(1658), Sir Kenelm Digby uses mechanistic principles to account for
the healing of wounds at a distance, the subject of an international controversy
in the seventeenth century. Seth Lobis examines Digby’s natural philosophy in the
light of his moral philosophy, showing that even as he defends the sympathetic
 action of the cure, Digby discredits the sympathetic interaction of human beings.
Lobis locates A Late Discourseand its stoic opposition to human sympathy in a
transitional period, one in which sympathy was increasingly conceived in human
terms. keywords: Abraham Tucker; John Locke; Thomas Browne; Earl of
Shaftesbury; sympathy and imaginative contagion
