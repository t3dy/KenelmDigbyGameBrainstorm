The book of Secretspublished by GeorgeHartman in r682 provides a rich
sample of the work being done in alchemy and chemical pharmacy during the
middle of the seventeenth century, when Digby was at hismost active and was
in constant contact with both the English and the continental groups which
were pursuing chemical studies. All of the items which aredated were collected
by Digby in the r650s and r660s, that most crucial period, when the Hartlib
circle was most productive, when IeFevre was teaching in Parisand subsequently
active in London, and when the Royal Societywas being founded. Moreover,
a few items reach back into that earlierperiod of Digby'scareer, to a time when
chemical activitieswere less likely to be organized by groups of men and had
still to depend on individualistic efforts. The recipe "To engender Cray-
fishes", for example, unquestionably comes from Digby's Gresham College
period in the r630s; his own favourite alchemical process probably does also;
and the process "To fix ~ of 5, or the Common ~" probably dates from the r630s
or r640s. A few minor pharmaceutical items seem to have been added by
Hartman at the time of publication.
Hartman fell heir to the collection in r665, upon Digby's death. As
Hartman explained in hisdedication of the volume to Lord Paston, he had
had the Honour and Happiness for several Years to Serve[SirK. D.],
beyond the Seas, as well as in England, and to attend on him more
particularly in the Production of many of hisincomparable Experiments,
and so to continue tillhis dying day; when he left with me those choice
things contained in thislittleTreatise.3
