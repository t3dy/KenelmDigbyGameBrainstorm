UNNOTICED WORDS AND SENSES
FROM SIR KENELM DIGBY
SIR KENELM DIGBY (1603-1665), the
Aristotelian philosopher and friend of Des-
cartes, is well represented in OED (2nd edn),
by more than 600 quotations from his works. Of
these, more than half come from his two chief
philosophical works, Two Treatises, published
at Paris in 1644 as one volume. Various short
titles are used in OED quotations from these two
works, The Nature of Bodies and The Nature of
Mans Soule. These abbreviated titles include
'Two Treat.' (17 quotations), 'Nat. Bodies'
(252), 'Treat. Bodies' (1), and 'Nat. Soul' (29),
'Mans Soul' (18), 'Of Man's Soul' (2). Many of
the quotations are the earliest in OED for that
word or sense, and some are the sole examples.
Obviously the Two Treatises has been rather
thoroughly excerpted, but I have found a few
examples in it that antedate the earliest use of a
word or sense in OED, or illustrate one not
given at all. In citing these the dates etc. found
in OED are given in square brackets, and the
page numbers at the start of the quotation are
Digby's (numbers higher than 346 belong to
the second of the Treatises, that on the soul).
Spelling follows the original exactly, except
that long esses are not reproduced.
